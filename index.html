Leasehold vs Freehold — what’s the difference?

Ownership legend: Freehold (foreign quota up to 49%) Leasehold (typically 30+30+30 yrs) *See legal note below
Freehold

What: Condominium ownership of unit + share in common property.

Foreign quota: Up to 49% of total sellable area can be registered to foreigners.

Pros: Most flexible for resale and financing. Cons: Quota may be sold out in popular projects.

Leasehold

What: Long-term registered lease (commonly 30 + 30 + 30 years).

Pros: Access to units when Freehold quota is unavailable; often simpler purchase flow.

Cons: Terms vary by project; renewals/assignments per registered lease & developer policy.

Legal note: Freehold availability is limited by the foreigner quota (up to 49% of condominium area). Leasehold conditions, renewals and assignments depend on the registered lease and developer policy. Always rely on the latest project documentation.
