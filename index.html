FAQ — Property Ownership in Thailand

Freehold: Condominium ownership (up to 49% of total sellable area for foreigners).

Leasehold: Registered lease, commonly 30 + 30 + 30 years.

Legal note: Lease renewals depend on developer policy. Always check project documentation.

Foreigners cannot own land directly, but can own condos (up to 49% of area) or lease land long-term (30+30+30 years).
Typical yields range from 5–9% annually in top tourist locations, depending on project and management.
Registered leaseholds are protected by law, but renewals must be documented in the developer’s policy.
